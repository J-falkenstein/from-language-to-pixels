Here is an example of an ASCII art representation of a chess board:

```
   a b c d e f g h
  +---------------+
8 |r|_|b|q|k|b|_|r| 8
7 |p|p|p|p|p|p|p|p| 7
6 |_|_|_|_|_|_|_|_| 6
5 |_|_|_|_|_|_|_|_| 5
4 |_|_|_|_|_|_|_|_| 4
3 |_|_|_|_|_|_|_|_| 3
2 |P|P|P|P|P|P|P|P| 2
1 |R|_|B|Q|K|B|_|R| 1
  +---------------+
   a b c d e f g h
```

In this representation, the letters and symbols are used to represent different chess pieces:

- r and R represent the black and white rooks, respectively
- b and B represent the black and white bishops, respectively
- q and Q represent the black and white queens, respectively
- k and K represent the black and white kings, respectively
- p and P represent the black and white pawns, respectively
- _ symbolizes an empty square on the board
